Delay Reasons

jaha jaha

Well why are planes delayed? There are 5 major delay categories into which all delays can be categorized into:

First there is carrier delays. Something in the schedules of the carrier did not add up and therefore a plane wasn't able to leave on time. Most common ones are maintenance and crew problems, aircraft cleaning, baggage loading, fueling, etc.

Weather delay is surprisingly small percentage of the total. If I had to guess why a plane was late, I would most likely say due to weather, which most likely isn't true. Weather delays only are marked if the weather conditions are extreme such as a hurricane, tornado, blizzard or other dangerous conditions.

NAS delay stands for National Aviation System delay. The NAS delays amount for the delays and cancellations attributable to the national aviation system that refer to a broad set of conditions, such as non-extreme weather conditions, airport operations, heavy traffic volume, and air traffic control.

Security delays are delays or cancellations caused by evacuation of a terminal or concourse, re-boarding of aircraft because of security breach, inoperative screening equipment and/or long lines in excess of 29 minutes at screening areas.

Late arriving aircraft delays are pretty self explanatory. The prevous flight was late, and now the next flight won't leave on time due to that.

Linear model

Masi Pallopää

On this linear regression model we are explaining arrive delay (min) with two variables: departure delay (min) and distance (km). It is selfexplanotary that if plane leaves late it will probably arrive late. It can be seen too in this model because departure delay's coefficient is 1.01. So if plane departures one minute late it arrives one minute late also meaning it will departure:arrive 1:1 by minutes.

Null hypothesis is that the distance and dep delay do not have relationship between arr delay. From summary we see that p-value for both departure delay and distance is 0, meaning we can reject the null hypothesis on significance level of 0.05. We conclude that distance and dep delay do have relationship between arr delay. The effect of distance is negative so from distance's coefficient , -0.0013, we see that further the plane flies the less it will arrive late. Altough the distance has a huge standard error , 3e-05, so our predictions of distance's effect to the arrive delay may not be accurate.

R^2 and F-statistic both have large values indicating the model fits well to the data. It is mainly because dep delay and arr delay have such strong relationship. By looking at the qq-plot we can conclude that the linear model is not suitable for this data as it should form a straight line, at least in the middle of the qq-plot. Therefore the conclusions from the model are inaccurate, statistically speaking.

Logistic model

Masi Pallopää

On this logistic regression model we are explaining arrive delay over 15 minutes with two variables: day of week and distance (km). If the plane arrives 15 minutes or more late to its destination it gets the value 1, else 0. Day of week is categorical factor and gets values from 1 to 7.

On significance level of 0.05 we can accept the null hypothesis of day of week but reject the distance. This means that on this model day of week has no effect on flights arriving late but distance has tiny amount of effect. Anyway both effects would be unnoticable because coefficient of day of week 0.002 is and distance's is 7e-05.

On this logistic regression model we notice the same thing that in the linear model: the model does not fit well. Logistic regression model's deviance is large, 7e+05, meaning the the model is not suitable for this data. Smaller the deviance, better fit for the model. All in all the conclusions from this logistic regression model are not very accurate.
